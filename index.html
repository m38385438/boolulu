About Canele

Canele, known as "Angel's Bell" in Taiwan

A canelé is a small French pastry flavored with rum and vanilla with a soft and tender custard center and a dark, thick caramelized crust.

It takes the shape of a small, striated cylinder up to five centimeters in height with a depression at the top. A specialty of the Bordeaux region of France, today it is widely available in pâtisseries in France and abroad.

4 Tips to bake Canele

The prepared batter should be placed in the refrigerator the day before

The batter after placing will undergo photosynthesis to make the canele more fragrant and organized.

Beeswax thickness should be mastered properly

Using organic, 100% odor-free beeswax works really well and adds a touch of flavor to the final canele. Applying too much beeswax can cause the canelé to bake unevenly or the crust to be too hard.

Carefully selected ingredients

Use whole milk, vanilla flavor use vanilla bean instead of vanilla extract, and choose dark rum for rum to make the flavor deeper.

The last step of the perfect canele

After cooking, quickly put into the freezer to reduce the traveling velocity of water keep the crust of the canele crisp.
